Biographical Information 
My application area is Mathematical Biology, and my dissertation topic was Mathematical Models for Facilitated Diffusion and Molecular Motors, which I studied under my advisor, Hong Qian. I sucessfully defended my Ph.D. on August 31, 2011. I also completed a M.Sc. in Applied Mathematics at the University of Washington in the fall of 2004. I received my B.A. from Macalester College (St. Paul, Minnesota) in 2002, with a double Math and Physics major. I grew up in Tacoma, Washington. I love the Northwest, and I am glad that I no longer have to deal with Minnesota summers or winters. My non-academic hobbies include playing soccer, rock climbing, photography, and reading.
